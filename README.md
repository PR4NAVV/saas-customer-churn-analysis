SaaS Subscription Churn Prediction & Customer Retention Analysis
Project Overview

Customer retention is one of the most critical success factors for subscription-based businesses. High customer churn directly impacts recurring revenue, customer lifetime value, and long-term business growth.

This project analyzes customer subscription data to identify the primary drivers of churn and recommend actionable strategies to improve customer retention. Using Power BI, interactive dashboards were developed to visualize customer behavior across contract types, payment methods, internet services, pricing, and customer tenure.

Business Problem

A subscription-based SaaS company has experienced increasing customer churn. The objective of this project is to identify the major factors contributing to customer attrition and provide data-driven recommendations to improve customer retention.

Objectives
Measure customer churn rate
Identify high-risk customer segments
Analyze contract type, payment method, pricing and service usage
Develop business recommendations to improve retention

Key Findings
Customers on month-to-month contracts exhibit the highest churn rate.
Electronic Check users experience the highest customer attrition.
Customers without Tech Support churn significantly more than those receiving support.
Fiber Optic customers demonstrate the highest churn among internet service categories.
New customers and customers within the High Monthly Charge Band are at the greatest risk of churn.
Business Recommendations
Encourage migration from month-to-month to annual contracts.
Improve onboarding for new customers.
Bundle Tech Support into subscription plans.
Review Fiber Optic pricing and service quality.
Improve the payment experience for Electronic Check users.
